Almora-Travel Guide: Best Place to Visit


Cultural capital of Kumaon region | June 24,2024

Almora, a picturesque hill town nestled in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and serene ambiance. Situated at an altitude of around 1,638 meters (5,374 feet) above sea level, Almora is a popular destination for those seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Almora is famous for its breathtaking views of the majestic Himalayan ranges, including the awe-inspiring peaks of Nanda Devi and Trishul. The town's strategic location in the Kumaon Hills offers visitors a panoramic vista of the snow-capped mountains, which are particularly striking during the early morning and evening hours when the light plays on the peaks. The town's cultural heritage is equally captivating, with a rich tapestry of traditions, festivals, and architectural marvels that showcase the region's deep-rooted connection to its past. Almora is home to several ancient temples, such as the Nanda Devi Temple and the Katarmal Sun Temple, which are revered for their historical significance and intricate carvings. One of the town's most famous attractions is the Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary, a lush, densely forested area that is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including the elusive Himalayan black bear, the Himalayan musk deer, and a variety of bird species. Visitors can explore the sanctuary's network of hiking trails, taking in the stunning vistas and immersing themselves in the tranquility of the natural world. The best time to visit Almora is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the natural surroundings are at their most vibrant. During the spring, the town is adorned with a profusion of blooming flowers, creating a riot of color that is a feast for the senses. In the autumn, the landscape is transformed by the changing foliage, with the hills and forests taking on a warm, golden hue.

• Main Places to Visit

1. Zero Point:

Nestled amidst the majestic Kumaon Himalayas, the Zero Point in Almora is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. This unique location, situated at an altitude of approximately 13,054 feet (3,978 meters), is renowned for its breathtaking panoramic vistas and its strategic importance as a military outpost during the Indo-China war. The journey to the Zero Point is an exhilarating experience in itself, as travelers navigate through winding mountain roads, passing by lush pine forests and picturesque villages. The final stretch of the journey involves a challenging hike, which rewards visitors with a stunning view of the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas, including the mighty Nanda Devi and Trishul. At the Zero Point, visitors can bask in the serene beauty of the surrounding landscape, which is characterized by rugged terrain, pristine meadows, and crystal-clear streams. The area is also home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including the elusive Himalayan black bear, the Himalayan musk deer, and a variety of bird species. In addition to its natural wonders, the Zero Point holds significant historical significance. During the Indo-China war in 1962, the area served as a strategic military outpost, and visitors can still see remnants of the old military bunkers and trenches that were used to defend the region. The best time to visit the Zero Point is during the summer months, when the weather is mild and the skies are clear, offering unobstructed views of the Himalayan peaks.


2. Dwarahat Village:

Nestled amidst the picturesque Kumaon hills, the quaint village of Dwarahat in Almora district of Uttarakhand is a hidden gem, steeped in rich cultural heritage and natural splendor. Situated at an average elevation of 1,829 meters (6,000 feet) above sea level, Dwarahat is a testament to the enduring traditions and timeless charm of the Kumaon region. The village's claim to fame lies in its numerous ancient temples, which dot the landscape and serve as a testament to the region's deep-rooted spiritual history. The most prominent of these is the Nagnath Temple, a revered 11th-century structure dedicated to the serpent deity Nag. Adorned with intricate carvings and intricate architectural elements, the temple is a must-visit for those seeking to immerse themselves in the region's sacred heritage. Aside from its religious significance, Dwarahat is also renowned for its stunning natural beauty. The village is surrounded by lush, verdant forests, which are home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, including the elusive Himalayan black bear and the majestic Himalayan musk deer. Visitors can explore the area's numerous hiking trails, which offer breathtaking vistas of the surrounding hills and snow-capped peaks. One of the highlights of a visit to Dwarahat is the opportunity to experience the vibrant local culture.


3. Bright End Corner:

Bright End Corner, Almora is a scenic spot located in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, India. It is situated at an altitude of approximately 1,638 meters (5,371 feet) above sea level and offers breathtaking views of the majestic Himalayan range. The area is known for its serene and peaceful environment, making it a popular destination for travelers and nature enthusiasts. The "Bright End" refers to the fact that the sun's rays are particularly vibrant and intense at this location, creating a stunning visual experience. One of the main attractions of Bright End Corner is the panoramic view it offers of the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayan mountains, including the famous Nanda Devi, the second-highest peak in India. Visitors can enjoy the stunning vistas from the various viewpoints and observation decks located around the area. In addition to the natural beauty, Bright End Corner is also home to several historical and cultural sites. The nearby Kasar Devi Temple, dedicated to the goddess Parvati, is a popular pilgrimage site and attracts devotees from all over the region. The temple is known for its intricate carvings and architecture, which reflect the rich cultural heritage of the Kumaon region. The area surrounding Bright End Corner is also dotted with quaint villages, where visitors can experience the traditional way of life in the Himalayan foothills.


4. Neeb Karori Baba Ashram:

Nestled amidst the majestic Himalayan peaks, the Neeb Karori Baba Ashram in Almora, Uttarakhand, stands as a beacon of spiritual solace and enlightenment. This sacred abode, dedicated to the revered saint Neeb Karori Baba, has become a hallowed destination for devotees and seekers alike, drawn by the profound teachings and the profound presence that permeates the very air of this hallowed place. The ashram, a serene oasis in the heart of the Kumaon hills, is a testament to the timeless wisdom and compassion of Neeb Karori Baba, a spiritual luminary whose life and teachings have inspired countless individuals across the globe. Within the ashram's tranquil confines, one can find a temple, meditation halls, and various sacred spaces that serve as a conduit for the devotee's connection with the divine. The temple, adorned with intricate carvings and vibrant murals, stands as a testament to the architectural and artistic prowess of the region, further enhancing the spiritual ambiance that envelops the ashram. Visitors to the Neeb Karori Baba Ashram are welcomed with open arms, invited to partake in the daily rituals, spiritual discourses, and meditative practices that form the core of the ashram's activities. The ashram's library, stocked with a wealth of spiritual literature, provides a serene haven for those seeking deeper insights and understanding.
