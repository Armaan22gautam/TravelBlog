Nainital: Best Place to Visit


Every visit to Nainital feels like a step into a dream | June 30,2024

Nainital, often referred to as the "Lake District of India," is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, situated at an elevation of about 2,084 meters (6,837 feet) above sea level. The town is built around a serene crescent-shaped lake, Naini Lake, which is dotted with vibrant pedal boats and surrounded by lush green hills, making it a favorite destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Nainital's lush landscapes, crystal-clear waters, and cool climate offer a refreshing escape from the sweltering heat of the plains, attracting tourists year-round. The mesmerizing Naini Lake is the centerpiece of the town and is surrounded by a charming promenade known as Mall Road. This bustling pathway is lined with shops, restaurants, and cafes, providing visitors with various options for shopping and dining. Popular activities at Naini Lake include boating, yachting, and taking leisurely walks along the lakeside. The lake is also steeped in mythology, believed to be the eye of the goddess Sati, and holds immense religious significance; hence, the town attracts pilgrims throughout the year. Apart from its natural beauty, Nainital is rich in history and colonial architecture. Established as a popular hill station during British rule in the 19th century, the town is dotted with charming colonial-era buildings such as the Governor's House (Raj Bhawan) and St. John's Church, which reflect its historical heritage. The charming cottages and bungalows, built during the British era, add to the town's old-world charm, making it a preferred destination for heritage enthusiasts. Wildlife enthusiasts can explore the Naina Devi Himalayan Bird Conservation Reserve, which is home to various bird species and rich flora and fauna. Additionally, the nearby Bhowali, a small town just 11 kilometers away, serves as a gateway to other scenic spots in the region, including Bhimtal, Sattal, and Naukuchiatal, each offering tranquil lakes and scenic beauty. The best time to visit Nainital is from late September to June, with peak tourist season occurring between March and June. During these months, the weather is pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F), making it ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Winter (December to February) is also a magical time to visit Nainital, especially for those who enjoy snowfall and winter sports, but temperatures can drop significantly, sometimes reaching as low as -3°C (26°F). However, during the monsoon season (July to September), heavy rains often lead to landslides, making travel challenging, so it is generally advisable to avoid visiting during this period.

• Main Places to Visit

1. Snow View Point:

Snow View Point is one of the most popular and breathtaking attractions in Nainital, situated at an altitude of 2,270 meters (7,450 feet) and approximately 3 kilometers (1.9 miles) from Mall Road. This viewpoint is renowned for its spectacular panoramic views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks, including the Nanda Devi, Nanda Kot, and Trishul ranges, making it a must-visit for both nature enthusiasts and photographers. Visitors can reach Snow View Point either by trekking through scenic forest paths or by taking a cable car ride from the base of the hill, offering a unique and exhilarating experience as it glides over the lush green landscapes and charming residences of Nainital. The viewpoint features well-maintained viewing platforms where visitors can relax, enjoy the stunning vistas, and capture picturesque photographs of the majestic mountains and the serene Naini Lake below. On clear days, the view can be absolutely mesmerizing, with the sun casting golden hues over the snow-clad peaks, creating a magical ambiance that highlights nature's beauty. The point also has several stalls and eateries that offer local snacks and beverages, allowing visitors to savor delicious treats while absorbing the views. Snow View Point is not just about the views; it also offers a rich cultural experience. The area has several small temples, including the popular Hanuman Temple, where visitors can offer prayers and enjoy the peaceful surroundings. The viewpoint is an excellent spot for birdwatching, as various species of birds inhabit the surrounding forests, making it a haven for wildlife enthusiasts.


2. Naina Peak:

Naina Peak, also known as China Peak, is the highest point in Nainital, situated at an elevation of about 2,615 meters (8,579 feet) above sea level. This prominent hill offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan ranges, lush valleys, and the picturesque Naini Lake below, making it a popular destination for trekkers, nature lovers, and photographers alike. The trek to Naina Peak is both invigorating and refreshing, usually starting from Mallital and taking approximately 2-3 hours of moderate hiking through dense forests filled with vibrant rhododendrons, oaks, and pines. The area surrounding Naina Peak is rich in biodiversity, providing opportunities for birdwatching, with various species commonly spotted in the wooded terrains. Upon reaching the summit, visitors are greeted with breathtaking vistas of snow-capped peaks such as Nanda Devi and Trishul, especially striking during sunrise and sunset when the mountains are bathed in golden light. A small temple dedicated to a local deity enhances the serene atmosphere at the peak, allowing visitors to pause for a moment of reflection and appreciation. Naina Peak is best visited between March and June, and September to December, as the weather during these months is pleasant, making the trek enjoyable. While the summer months attract many tourists, the winter season offers a different charm, with occasional snowfall transforming the landscape into a winter wonderland.


3. Tiffin Top:

Tiffin Top, also known as Dorothy's Seat, is a serene and picturesque viewpoint located in Nainital, situated at an elevation of about 2,292 meters (7,487 feet) above sea level. Named in honor of an English artist named Dorothy Kellet, who lost her life in a plane crash, this charming spot was built as a memorial by her friends and family. The journey to Tiffin Top involves a scenic trek of approximately 4 kilometers (2.5 miles) from Mall Road, offering trekkers stunning views of the lush pine forests and breathtaking vistas of the Himalayan range. The significance of Tiffin Top lies not only in its scenic beauty but also in its tranquil surroundings, making it a popular destination for picnics and leisurely outings. The viewpoint offers mesmerizing panoramic views of the Naini Lake, the surrounding hills, and the distant snow-capped peaks, creating a perfect backdrop for photography and relaxation. The area is marked by a stone bench that commemorates Dorothy and provides visitors a perfect spot to sit and soak in the enchanting scenery. Tiffin Top is surrounded by a rich diversity of flora and fauna, with the forests hosting various bird species, making it a haven for nature lovers and birdwatchers. The best time to visit Tiffin Top is between March to June and September to December, when the weather is pleasant for trekking and the views are particularly stunning.


4. Raj Bhawan Governor's House:

Raj Bhawan, also known as the Governor's House, is an iconic building located in Nainital, Uttarakhand, serving as the official residence of the Governor of the state. Built during the British colonial era in 1880, the structure reflects a distinctive blend of Scottish Gothic architecture and Indian styles, featuring intricate woodwork and beautifully landscaped gardens that add to its charm. Set against the backdrop of lush green hills, the building is surrounded by sprawling lawns filled with vibrant flowers and towering trees, offering a refreshing atmosphere for visitors and residents alike. Raj Bhawan is not only an architectural marvel but also holds historical significance, serving as a summer retreat for British officials. The sprawling estate spans approximately 200 acres, encompassing beautiful walking paths, a golf course, and a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains and Naini Lake. The building has been well-preserved and showcases various artifacts and memorabilia from the British period, providing a glimpse into the region’s colonial history. Visitors can enjoy guided tours of the premises, which include insights into its history, architecture, and the role it plays in the governance of Uttarakhand. The house also features an impressive collection of artwork, antique furniture, and photographs that depict the rich heritage of the region.
